  1. General Facts
    • Brigham Young University (BYU) is a private, non-profit, coeducational research university located in Provo, Utah, United States.
    • Founded in 1875, it is owned and operated by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS Church).
  2. Stats
    • BYU offers more than 250 undergraduate and graduate degree programs, including a variety of professional and specialized degrees.
    • The university is home to more than 33,000 students from all 50 states and over 100 countries.
    • BYU is ranked in the top 50 universities in the U.S. by U.S. News & World Report and is consistently ranked as one of the nation's top universities for value.
  3. Other Things to Note
    • BYU is known for its excellent academics and its high-quality faculty.
    • The university emphasizes a strict code of honor and a commitment to service and community.
    • As a religiously affiliated university, BYU takes a unique approach to education, blending academic studies with spiritual growth and development.
